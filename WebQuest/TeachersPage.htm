Introduction

Math Public Relations is a Webquest dedicated to making students write good things about math in the hopes that they may come to believe what they are saying about Mathematics. Too often students are left feeling distant from math and feeling like it does not apply to their life.

Learners

This Webquest is geared towards grades 8 - 12. It may be important to expose students to other mathematicians and other uses for math before they get to the point of not having to take a math class. For that reason, it may be more useful in 8th or 9th grade. It still has value for older students. They probably enjoy math and the public relations paper would not be in terms of them so much as it would be about convincing other people.

Standards

The standards that are addressed in this Webquest are as follows according to New York State Standards:

Standards for Science and Mathematics

Standard 2: Students will access, generate, process, and transfer information using appropriate technologies.

Standard 3: Students will understand mathematics and become mathematically confident by communicating and reasoning mathematically, by applying mathematics in real-world settings, and by solving problems through the integrated study of number systems, geometry, algebra, data analysis, probability, and trigonometry.

Standard 6: Students will understand the relationships and common themes that connect mathematics, science, and technology and apply the themes to these and other areas of learning.

Standard 7: Students will apply the knowledge and thinking skills of mathematics, science, and technology to address real-life problems and make informed decisions.

Standards for Art

Standard 3: Responding to and Analyzing Works of Art Students will respond critically to a variety of works in the arts, connecting the individual work to other works and to other aspects of human endeavor and thought.

Process

In Math Public Relations, there are three steps.

The first step is to repaint the face of mathematicians. The students put faces and people to math that has been done through the use of mathematicians. During this process students will find a mathematician that is either a woman or a person of color. This is meant to combat stereotypes and the sense that the field of Mathematics is dominated by white men.

The second step is to bridge the gap between Mathematics and Art. This will be done by having the students find a piece of art that uses mathematics or has mathematical meaning. Students are suggested to use M. C. Escher but there are other mathematical works of art that can be used.

The Third and final step is to address the practicality of math. This is to give students a sense of why they are learning Mathematics. This is meant to draw on the practicality of Mathematics to get students interested in the course content.
